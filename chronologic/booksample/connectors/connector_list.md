List of connectors used to categorize sentences and clauses
===========================================================

CAUSE/REASON/PURPOSE
--------------------

1. Sentence-starting patterns.

as first word: n/a
in first five words: justification, rationale, purpose, cause, because (not preceded by comma)
if the *previous* sentence began with "why" and ended with a question mark

2. Clause-starting patterns.

following a comma or semicolon: because, since, as, in order that, in order to, for, seeing that, owing to, inasmuch, forasmuch

EFFECT/INFERENCE
----------------

1. Sentence-starting patterns.

as first word: so, hence
in first five words: thus, therefore, thence, accordingly, consequence, result, effect, consequently, it follows that

2. Clause-starting patterns:
following a comma or semicolon: so
after a comma, in sentences beginning with if: then 

CONTRAST/CONTRADICTION
----------------------

1. Sentence-starting patterns:
as first word: but, yet
in first five words: however, nevertheless

2. Clause-starting patterns:
after a comma or semicolon: but, however, nevertheless

CONDITIONAL CLAUSES
-------------------

after a comma: if, provided that, insofar as

CONCESSIVE CLAUSES
------------------
after a comma: although, though, even though, even if, albeit, while
